Engineering Method, Not Marketing Claims.

Eonix Systems is built around practical embedded engineering: clear architecture, hardware-aware firmware, early testing, and handover details that make the next build easier.

Why Eonix

The work starts by understanding the whole embedded system, then narrowing execution around the risks that can stop the hardware from working reliably.

Architecture First

Power rails, controllers, sensors, communication buses, connectors, and protection strategy are planned before the board becomes hard to change.

Hardware + Firmware Together

Firmware decisions are tied to the schematic, pinout, bus timing, startup sequence, test points, and expected failure modes.

Testable From Day One

Bring-up checklists, debug signals, firmware test hooks, and validation notes are part of the engineering plan.

Manufacturing-Aware Design

Layouts, BOMs, assembly notes, and handover files are prepared so prototype work can move toward repeatable builds.

Deterministic Systems

Communication, timing, startup behavior, and error handling are designed to be understandable and repeatable under test.

Clear Technical Handover

Teams receive design files, assumptions, known risks, test notes, and revision guidance in a form they can continue using.

How This Helps

Eonix is not positioned as a large product company. The current focus is engineering services for teams that need reliable electronics, practical debugging, and cleaner handover from prototype to next build.

Fewer late hardware/firmware surprises
Better board bring-up visibility
Cleaner manufacturing preparation
More usable documentation after delivery

Founder-Led Focus

Eonix Systems was founded by Rishabh Jain with a focus on embedded systems, PCB design, power electronics, and robotics hardware.

The company is intentionally services-first today: support teams with board design, firmware, bring-up, debugging, integration, and manufacturing handover while the longer-term modular CAN-based ecosystem remains an engineering direction, not a finished product claim.
