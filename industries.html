Sectors

Where The Work Fits

The industry label matters less than the engineering condition: boards that must be built, firmware that must be debugged, and systems that must survive real use.

Robotics

Controller electronics, sensor interfaces, power distribution, and communication networks for mobile or fixed robotic systems.

  • Control boards and motor-adjacent electronics
  • Power and communication validation
  • Field-focused revision support
  • Bring-up and test planning

Industrial Automation

Embedded controllers, interface boards, monitoring hardware, and revisions that need predictable electrical behavior in operating equipment.

  • Signal and power interface design
  • Controller electronics and IO handling
  • Robust firmware integration
  • Documentation for maintenance and update cycles

IoT

Connected embedded products where low-power decisions, communications, board compactness, and firmware stability all matter at once.

  • Sensor and telemetry boards
  • Wireless-ready hardware review
  • Firmware for data and control paths
  • Small-batch manufacturing preparation

Defence & Aerospace

Electronics development support for programs that require disciplined validation, cautious public positioning, and documentation another engineer can follow.

  • Revision-controlled board work
  • Structured validation support
  • Careful power and interface handling
  • Technical handover documentation

Research Labs

Experimental electronics, measurement systems, and rapid prototypes that need quicker loops between ideas, test results, and the next build.

  • Custom data and instrumentation boards
  • Rapid prototype iterations
  • Debug-ready firmware baselines
  • Bench validation workflows

Consumer Electronics

Compact embedded products that need manufacturable board files, stable firmware, and practical planning around assembly and testing.

  • Compact PCB revision support
  • Prototype to pilot-build handover
  • Interface and sensor integration
  • Production-ready outputs
